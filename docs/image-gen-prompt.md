# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include fill-height tables, pitch maps, glass dimensions, tray dimensions, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

Photorealistic 3/4 product photograph of a finished glass harp as a tuned set: a row of clear stemmed wine glasses arranged in a gentle arc on a slim spill-safe tray with a soft non-slip pad, each glass holding a different amount of water so the levels step subtly across the row and read instantly as a tuned set rather than a dinner arrangement. The signature feature is the line of upright polished rims meant to be circled by a wet finger — thin-walled clear glass bowls lightly spaced for hand access, lifted just clear of a matte neutral tray, with small removable moisture-safe label tabs at each glass base (blank, no printed text or numbers). A folded towel and a small wetting vessel are staged nearby as part of the playing system. Clear glass reflections and water surfaces catching light, realistic tabletop scale from the stems and hand-spacing gaps, clean neutral studio background, soft directional studio lighting raking across the rims, ~50mm lens, high detail, no readable labels.

## In-Context Prompt

Photorealistic scene of the glass harp staged on a workbench or low performance table in a quiet studio or recital room, natural side light through the glass set, a player's wetted fingertip just touching one rim mid-arc with fine concentric ripples on that glass's water surface from the stick-slip excitation, the other glasses sitting securely at varied fill levels along the spaced row on a damped tray. A small water pitcher, a damp towel, and faint condensation on a couple of bowls tell the story of an in-progress measured-fill tuning session. Realistic anatomically plausible hand, delicate reflections, shallow depth of field with the touched rim sharp and far glasses softening into bokeh, environmental storytelling about fill workflow and spill control, concept visualization only.

## Detail Prompt

Photorealistic macro close-up of the signature glass-harp mechanism: a single thin clear wine-glass rim with a wetted fingertip riding it, the water meniscus inside catching a thin highlight and trembling into concentric ripples, the polished rim crisp and intact, faint internal reflections and glass-wall thickness visible, droplets beading on the outer wall. A small blank moisture-safe label tab clings near the glass base, deliberately textless. Soft raking light grazes the rim to reveal surface tension and glass thickness, neighboring glasses softly blurred, a stable pad or tray surface below, high detail, no fill marks or pitch callouts, no printed numbers.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, readable note/pitch labels, dimension callouts or fill-height gauges, impossible identical-pitch claims, floating glasses, broken glass, spilling water onto electronics, extra or malformed fingers, or turning the setup into a rotating glass armonica.
